## What are Chemical Relaxers?

Chemical hair relaxers are products that are used to straighten curly or kinky hair. These products work by breaking down the protein bonds in hair, which makes it easier to straighten. The chemicals found in hair relaxers can be harsh, and prolonged use can cause damage to the hair and scalp.
The history of chemical hair relaxers can be traced back to the early 1900s when Madam C.J. Walker, the first self-made female millionaire in America, developed a formula to straighten hair. However, it was not until the 1940s that the first commercial hair relaxer, called "Curl Free," was introduced.
Over the years, hair relaxers have evolved, and new formulations have been developed. Today, there are many different types of hair relaxers available on the market, including lye and no-lye relaxers.
While hair relaxers and hair straighteners are often used interchangeably, they are not the same thing. Chemical hair relaxers permanently change the hair's texture by breaking its protein bonds, while chemical hair straighteners provide a temporary straightening effect and use milder chemicals to alter the hair's texture.

## Dangers Associated with Chemical Hair Relaxers

Although chemical hair relaxers can effectively straighten hair, they carry serious health hazards.
Chemical hair relaxers are capable of causing both short-term and long-term damage to the hair and scalp.
Following are examples of the potential short- and long-term risks connected with chemical hair relaxers:

* **Hair breakage and loss:** Chemical hair relaxers can weaken hair and cause it to break or fall out. Prolonged use can cause long-lasting damage to the hair follicles, leading to hair loss and thinning.
* **Scalp irritation and itching:** Chemical hair relaxers contain harsh chemicals that can irritate the scalp, causing itching, redness, and soreness.
* **Allergic reactions:** Some individuals may be allergic to the chemicals found in chemical hair relaxers, leading to allergic reactions such as hives, swelling, and difficulty breathing.
* **Chemical pneumonia:** Inhaling the fumes from chemical hair relaxers can lead to chemical pneumonia, a condition characterized by inflammation of the lungs.
* **Cancer:** Chemical hair relaxers have been linked to an increased risk of cancer. Formaldehyde and parabens are two examples of chemicals found in some hair relaxers that have been shown to have carcinogenic properties.

## How to Protect Yourself from the Risks of Chemical Hair Relaxers

If you choose to use a chemical hair relaxer, there are actions you may do to protect yourself from the potential risks:

* **Research the product:** Before employing a chemical hair relaxer, investigate the product and its contents to ascertain its safety. Search for items that do not contain harmful ingredients such as sodium hydroxide.
* **Follow the instructions:** Always strictly adhere to the directions included with the product. Do not leave the product on your hair for longer than advised and avoid using the product excessively.
* **Test the product:** Before using a product on your hair, perform a patch test. Apply a tiny amount of the product to a small section of your scalp and observe for 24 hours for any bad reactions.
* **Use protective gear:** Employ protective gloves and a protective cape to avoid skin and clothing contact.
* **Contact a professional:** To guarantee that the hair relaxer is done correctly and safely, consider having a professional stylist do it for you.
