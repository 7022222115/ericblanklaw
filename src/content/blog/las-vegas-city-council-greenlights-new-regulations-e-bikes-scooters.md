**Las Vegas City Council Greenlights New Regulations for E‑Bikes & Scooters**
The Las Vegas City Council has approved a new ordinance—introduced by Councilwoman Nancy Brune—to govern the use of electric bicycles and scooters within city-managed parks, plazas, and recreational areas. These measures, closely modeled on Clark County’s rules to ensure consistent enforcement, will take effect on **October 1, 2025**.
Under the ordinance, a maximum **15 mph speed limit** applies across all designated city facilities. Devices must be outfitted with essential safety gear—**white lamps, reflectors**, and an **audible warning device** capable of being heard from at least 100 feet away.
Minors are required to wear **helmets**, and riders must not engage in any reckless conduct, including **wheelies or stunts**, both in parks and on public plazas. Additionally, riding e-bikes or e‑-scooters‑ is prohibited on the sidewalks of **Fremont Street from Main to Seventh Streets**.
Enforcement will be carried out by **city marshals** and the **Metropolitan Police Department**, with fines structured as follows:

* **$150** for the first offense;
* **$300** for the second; and
* **$500** for subsequent violations.

City leaders underscored that **safety** was the driving force behind the regulations. Councilman **Brian Knudsen** praised the legislation as “great,” while **Public Safety Chief Jason Potts** stressed the dual goals of protecting riders and preserving park turf.
