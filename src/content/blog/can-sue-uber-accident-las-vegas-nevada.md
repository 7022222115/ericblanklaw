## When Can You Sue Uber for a Las Vegas Accident?

While Uber provides commercial [insurance coverage](https://www.uber.com/us/en/drive/insurance/) for its drivers while they're on a trip with passengers, suing Uber might be an option in specific situations:

* **Uber Driver Negligence:** If the Uber driver's actions caused the accident, such as texting while driving, speeding, or driving under the influence, you may have a case against Uber.
* **Shared Liability:** Nevada's comparative negligence law assigns fault as a percentage. Even if another driver caused the accident, Uber might still share some liability depending on the circumstances. A [rideshare accident lawyer in Las Vegas](/practice-areas/rideshare-accidents) can help determine this.
* **Improper Background Checks:** If Uber failed to conduct proper background checks on a driver with a history of reckless driving or safety violations, and that driver caused the accident, you might be able to sue Uber.

## Damages You Can Recover After an Uber Accident in Las Vegas

The aftermath of an accident can be physically, emotionally, and financially draining.  If you've been injured in an Uber accident, you may be entitled to compensation for various damages, including:

* **Medical expenses:** This covers all costs associated with treating your injuries, including doctor visits, hospital stays, medications, and rehabilitation.
* **Lost wages:** If your injuries prevent you from working, you can recover compensation for the wages you lose during your recovery period.
* **Lost earning capacity:** In some cases, injuries can permanently affect your ability to work and earn a living. You may be entitled to compensation for future lost wages.
* **Decreased quality of life:** Injuries can significantly impact your daily life, making it difficult to perform everyday tasks or participate in activities you once enjoyed. You can recover compensation for this loss.
* **Pain and suffering:** This covers the physical and emotional pain you experience due to your injuries.
* **Punitive damages:**  In rare cases, the court may award punitive damages to punish the at-fault party for gross negligence or intentional misconduct.

## Steps to Take If You've Been Involved in an Uber Accident

The aftermath of an Uber accident can be confusing and overwhelming.  Here are the crucial steps to take to protect yourself and your rights:

### Ensure Your Safety and Seek Medical Attention

* **Call 911 immediately:** This is the most important step. Don't wait for the Uber driver to call for help.
* **Seek medical attention:** Even if your injuries seem minor, get checked out by a doctor or visit an urgent care facility.  Las Vegas has several medical centers equipped to handle accident victims.
* **Keep medical records and receipts:**  Hold onto all documentation related to your injuries and treatment.

### Document the Accident Scene

* **Gather information:**  Exchange contact details with all drivers involved, including the Uber driver. Get the names and insurance information of any witnesses if possible.
* **Take pictures and videos:** While prioritizing safety, take pictures and videos of the accident scene from various angles. This should include damage to all vehicles involved, any visible injuries (once safe to do so), and surrounding traffic conditions.
* **Report the accident through the Uber app:**  Report the accident as soon as possible following the in-app instructions.

### Protect Your Legal Rights

* **Don't discuss the accident:** Avoid discussing the accident with anyone except the police and medical personnel. Don't admit fault or make any statements that could be misconstrued by insurance companies.
* **Don't accept settlement offers:**  Insurance companies may try to settle your claim quickly for a low amount. Don't accept anything without first consulting with a Las Vegas Uber accident attorney.

## Unique Challenges of Uber Accidents in Las Vegas

While Uber accidents share similarities with regular car accidents, there are unique complexities:

* **Multiple parties involved:** Depending on the situation, Uber, the driver, and the other driver involved might all share liability.
* **Shared liability:** Figuring out who's to blame for an Uber accident can be complex. There are several parties involved, and they might all try to shift responsibility. The good news is that Nevada follows a [comparative negligence law](https://law.justia.com/codes/nevada/2010/title3/chapter41/nrs41-141.html). This means you can still recover damages as long as you're less than 50% at fault for the accident. As a passenger, you're unlikely to be considered mainly responsible unless you were distracting the driver. An experienced lawyer can help navigate this process and ensure Uber's fair share of responsibility.
* **Proving Uber driver negligence:** Building a strong case often requires evidence beyond your own testimony.
