**FAQs About DACA Renewal**

1. **Who is eligible to renew DACA?**
   You may renew your DACA status if:

* You currently have DACA and it hasn’t expired for more than one year.
* You have not left the U.S. without advance parole since your last renewal.
* You have no significant criminal history or other disqualifying factors.

2. **When should I renew my DACA?**
   We recommend applying for renewal **120 to 150 days (4 to 5 months)** before your current DACA expires. Acting early reduces the risk of gaps in your status.
3. **What happens if I don’t renew my DACA in time?**
   If your DACA status lapses, you lose protection from deportation and work authorization. Renewing as soon as possible minimizes these risks.
4. **How long does the renewal process take?**
   On average, processing can take 4 to 6 months. Submitting your application early ensures you remain covered during the review period.
5. **What documents are needed for renewal?**
   You’ll need:

* Completed Form I-821D, Form I-765, and Form I-765WS.
* Two passport-style photos.
* Copies of your current employment authorization card and supporting documents from your initial DACA application.
